Michael Jordan, born on February 17, 1963, is a renowned American businessman and former professional basketball player, widely regarded as one of the greatest basketball players of all time. He played 15 seasons in the NBA, primarily with the Chicago Bulls, where he won six championships and earned numerous accolades, including five MVP awards and ten scoring titles. Jordan's impact on the game extended beyond his on-court achievements; he was instrumental in popularizing basketball globally during the 1980s and 1990s.

Jordan's basketball journey began at the University of North Carolina, where he won a national championship in 1982. He was drafted by the Bulls in 1984 and quickly became a star, known for his scoring ability, defensive skills, and competitive nature. His nicknames, "Air Jordan" and "His Airness," reflect his exceptional leaping ability and style of play. After an initial retirement in 1993, he briefly pursued a career in baseball before returning to the NBA in 1995, leading the Bulls to another three consecutive championships.

In addition to his basketball career, Jordan is a successful businessman, having built a lucrative brand around his name, particularly through Nike's Air Jordan sneakers. He became the first billionaire player in NBA history and has been involved in various business ventures, including ownership stakes in the Charlotte Hornets and a NASCAR team.

Jordan's legacy includes numerous records, two-time Olympic gold medals, and induction into multiple halls of fame. He is celebrated not only for his athletic prowess but also for his influence on sports marketing and culture. His philanthropic efforts and contributions to various causes further enhance his status as a global icon. As of 2024, his net worth is estimated at $3.2 billion.
